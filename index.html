Canny Edge Detection

This project aimed to optimize the Canny edge detection algorithm by implementing it across multiple parallel computing platforms, including OpenMP, CUDA, and OpenCL. The objective was to compare their performance in terms of execution time, efficiency, and resource utilization on both multi-core CPUs and NVIDIA GPUs. Using OpenMP for parallel processing, CUDA for GPU acceleration, and OpenCL for cross-platform compatibility, the results showed significant performance improvements, with CUDA achieving the highest speedup, followed by OpenCL and OpenMP. The project also highlighted the trade-offs between CUDA's superior GPU performance and OpenCL's flexibility for heterogeneous computing environments.

Safe Chew: Food Spoilage Detection

SAFE-CHEW, short for "Spoilage Alert & Food Evaluation for Consumable Health and Environmental Wellness," is a portable system designed to detect food spoilage in real-time, ensuring consumers receive safe and high-quality food. Using the ESP32 microcontroller and MQ2/MQ3 gas sensors, it continuously monitors spoilage indicators like methane and alcohol emissions, signaling when food items may no longer be safe to consume. Real-time alerts are displayed through an intuitive display unit, making SAFE-CHEW a reliable, user-friendly tool that promotes food safety and reduces waste. Its innovative approach aligns with global sustainability goals, enhancing public health and environmental wellness.
